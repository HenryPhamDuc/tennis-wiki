# Backhand Biomechanics

Backhand biomechanics differ from the forehand in that both arms typically drive the swing (two-handed) or the single arm must generate all force with precise timing (one-handed). The kinetic chain still applies, with the hips and shoulders rotating to generate power, but the involvement of both arms changes the stability and timing.
