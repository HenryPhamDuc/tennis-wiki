# Forward Head Training: 80/20 Protocol

## Câu hỏi gốc

> *What if I train with a forward head on purpose — would that build better balance?*

## Câu trả lời ngắn

> **Yes — nhưng chỉ khi bạn xem tư thế cúi đầu như một chiếc áo tạ, không phải tư thế mặc định.**

## Cái gì thực sự cải thiện

### 1. Sensory reweighting

- Mất horizon → brain up-weights vestibular + ankle proprioception
- Nghiên cứu: vestibular training **enhances multisensory integration** in postural control (Sensory Organization Test), kể cả healthy adults
- Bạn dạy hệ thống giữ thẳng khi vision unreliable

### 2. Habituation to pitch

- Bending forwards = standard habituation drill
- Provoke dizziness, let symptoms settle, repeat, progress
- Sau vài tuần, cùng độ cúi gây ít chao đảo hơn

### 3. Specific carryover

- Stroke rehab: induced forward-tilt training restores ambulation in patients stuck in backward retropulsion
- Teaches nervous system **chịu đựng và kiểm soát trọng tâm bị đổ về trước**
- Parkinson's: vestibular-oriented balance work **improves postural control** hơn traditional balance work

→ Nếu sport/life yêu cầu nhìn xuống (wrestling, BJJ, MTB, plumbing dưới bồn) → ngắn + controlled forward-head practice giúp bớt hoảng khi mất horizon

## Cái gì tệ đi nếu bạn sống luôn ở đó

(Đã note từ [05.1_Head_Position_Control_Loop](05.1_head_position_control_loop.md): "no bend of the knees")

- VOR tries to stabilize vision during head movements by adjusting eye muscles, but **hits its limit** → **Dynamic visual acuity drops**, miss fast cues
- Brain chọn **stiffness over fluidity**:
  - Healthy people thực ra có head movements **biến thiên nhiều hơn** vestibular patients
  - Higher sample entropy = more adaptability, không phải less
  - Tự tập lock head + lock knees = tập **ngược lại adaptability**
- Neck extensors fatigue, thoracic flexion increases, baseline neutral calibration **drifts**
- Giỏi hơn khi bị nghiêng, **tệ hơn khi đứng thẳng**

> That's why rehab frameworks **never start with forward tilt**
> Họ progress qua 6 categories:
> 
> 1. static
> 2. compliant surface
> 3. weight shifting
> 4. modified center of gravity
> 5. gait
> 6. VOR
> 
> Chỉ thêm head-pitch challenges **sau khi neutral baseline solid**

## Cách dùng: 80/20

| Tỉ lệ                | Hoạt động                                                         |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **80%** balance work | Neutral head, horizon available (build VOR gain + ankle strategy) |
| **20%**              | Deliberate horizon removal (short, progressive, measurable)       |

## 4-step progression (từ clinic protocol)

### 1. Seated pitch holds

- Sit, tuck chin 20° down, hold 30 sec while breathing
- No sway allowed
- 3 reps
- **Habituation, not strength**

### 2. Standing weight shifts, nose down

- Feet hip-width, pitch head, shift weight slow side-to-side
- **Keep knees soft on purpose** — override the lock reflex
- 1 minute

### 3. Gait với brief look-down

- 10 steps neutral → 3 steps looking at feet → lift
- Repeat
- **Training transition, not tilt itself**

### 4. Eyes-closed variant

- Same as #2, but eyes closed for 10 seconds
- Removed **both** horizon **and** detailed vision
- Force vestibular + proprioception

### Stop rule

> **Dừng khi triệu chứng lắng xuống, không phải khi kiệt sức**
> 
> Nếu lingering dizziness, nausea, neck pain → not adaptation, that's **overload** → back off to neutral

## Bottom line

> **Train forward head on purpose** = builds specific kind of balance (stability without horizon)
> 
> **Nhưng KHÔNG tạo better balance overall** nếu biến nó thành home base
> 
> Use như **spice**: small doses force vestibular system to work → return to neutral to integrate gains
> 
> **Head leads, but should lead back to level most of the time**
