# Discus Throw — Kinetic Sequence (Áp dụng cho Tennis)

## Nguồn

Trong track and field, **discus throw** là dynamic event cao, power generated qua full **1.5-turn rotational sequence**. Thay vì một "position" đơn lẻ, thrower di chuyển qua series of critical, high-precision body alignments để maximize centripetal force + kinetic transfer.

## The Kinetic Sequence (5 phases)

### 1. The Starting Position (The Wind-Up)

- **Back of the circle**
- Thrower đứng ở back, facing **completely away** from target
- Feet spread slightly wider than shoulder-width
- 1-2 preliminary swings (wind-ups) để:
  - Establish rhythm
  - Shift CoG over **left leg**

### 2. The Entry Phase (The Turn)

- Weight shifts dynamically to **left foot**
- Left knee + toe pivot toward direction of throw
- Right leg **sweeps wide** để generate maximum angular momentum
- Throwing arm stays **trailed far behind** the body → tạo **stretch-shortening cycle** ở core

### 3. The Airborne / Drive Phase

- Push off left foot → **briefly airborne** as drive across center of ring
- Right foot targets **exact center** of throwing circle
- Land on **ball of foot** với knee bent để absorb + re-direct force

### 4. The Power Position (Maximum tension)

**Most critical structural alignment**

- Right foot anchored in **center of ring**
- Left foot plants quickly near **front edge** (tạo "block")
- **Hips turned forward**, upper torso + throwing arm still twisted **backward**
- → Tạo **immense torque** across torso

### 5. The Delivery & Release

- **Right hip snaps violently forward and upward**
- Energy transfers up through core
- **Left side acts as rigid brake** (blocking) → forces all velocity into releasing arm
- Release at shoulder height, mechanical launch angle **34° to 40°**

## Biomechanical Mastery: Static vs Dynamic Reality

### Discobolus statue

- Famous Myron's ancient Greek statue
- Artistic nhưng represents **static, historical variation**, not modern rotational mechanics

### Modern biomechanics

- Dựa vào **unbroken sequence of centripetal acceleration**
- Move từ **low, stable base** at entry → **high, explosive extension** at release

## 3 Core Mechanical Checkpoints

### 1. The Long Lever

- Throwing arm phải **completely relaxed and extended** throughout rotation
- Tensing bicep or pulling discus inward → **shortens the radius**
- → Drastically reduces linear velocity at release

### 2. Torque Generation (Hip-Shoulder Separation)

- **Lower body must always lead upper body**
- In power position:
  - Hips face target
  - Shoulders remain turned back
- → Stretches large core muscles → snap back như massive rubber band

### 3. The Dynamic Block

- **Left leg must plant firmly and straighten completely** right before delivery
- Instantly halts forward linear momentum of lower body
- → Transfers all accumulated kinetic energy **directly up** into chest, shoulder, arm

## Mapping sang tennis (từ sổ tay của tôi)

Notebook sketches không phải static pose như Discobolus. Đó là dynamic checkpoints, especially transition vào + ra khỏi Power Position.

### 4 sketches mapping

#### 1. Back View: "Side Bend at waist"

- = **Entry Phase** + early **Power Position**
- Right leg sweeps wide, throwing arm stays trailed far behind
- Trail **không phải forward bow**. Đó là **lateral side bend over right hip**
- Cảnh báo: đừng bend forward at waist during wind-up
- Correct: torso tilting **away from direction of throw** → keeps CoG over left leg before drive

#### 2. Top View: "CoG behind the Knee Line"

- = Balance rule cho cả Entry + Power Position
- Entry: shift weight dynamically to left foot + pivot
- Power Position: right foot anchored center, left foot plants near front
- Cả 2 moments: CoG phải stay **behind knees**, not over toes
- Nếu CoG drift forward → lose ability to block → kill centripetal force

#### 3. Body Bend Posture: "Same motion as a discus thrower"

- = **Power Position** torque checkpoint
- Hips turned forward, upper torso + throwing arm still twisted backward
- Coiled with arms wide, spine tilted
- = hip-shoulder separation
- Lower body leads, upper body lags

#### 4. Side View: "Shift to the left side"

- = **Delivery & Release** + **Dynamic Block**
- Right hip snaps forward and up
- Left side plants + straightens để brake
- Torso leaning left, arm hanging down after release
- Post-block position: weight transferred fully onto left leg, but spine still tilted away from throw

## Key cue on margin

> "Key: Instead of bending forward at the backswing phase, bend backward to..."

= Exactly the difference between static Discobolus idea and modern rotational mechanics

- **Bending forward**: shortens lever, collapses left side, moves CoG over toes. Lose radius, can't block.
- **Bending backward (side-bending away from target) while staying behind knee line**: keeps throwing arm long + relaxed, maintains hip-shoulder separation, lets left leg act as rigid brake → all velocity goes into discus
