# Lý thuyết cốt lõi — Automation

## Nguyên lý "đúng grip"

> The correct grip is the grip that you **don't feel having to adjust** the racquet face angle at contact — it will happen naturally at contact and turns the racquet head from the open position to close position **automatically**.

## 3 hệ quả

### 1. Pre-compensation beats manipulation

- Most rec players try to square face at contact
- → Bạn làm ngược lại:
  - Forehand: start **palm-up** so natural pronation through swing brings it vertical
  - Backhand: start **palm-down** ("see the watch") so bạn không flip open
- → Less timing to mess up

### 2. Time dictates the engine

- **No time** (return, quick volley): small wrist flip — thumb goes from top to bottom, face closes in 1 motion
- **Time** (baseline rally): big shoulder loop + lock the wrist
- → Ở 52 tuổi: bảo vệ wrist + elbow

### 3. Volleys and slice serves = waiting games

> "wait for the ball with the face already in the right position"

- Bạn không swinging to create angle
- Bạn **holding it** + letting the ball arrive
- → Why good volleyers look like they do nothing

## Common trap

> Mixing the two loops:
> 
> - Big shoulder loop + compact wrist flip at end
> - Face closes too much → spray left
> - "Keep wrist position stable" for big loop = guardrail

## Practical way to feel it

### In Surrey

1. Stand in front of mirror, no ball
2. Take backswing, freeze, check palm
3. Forehand: palm to sky
4. Backhand: watch to sky

### For compact

- Practice thumb-top to thumb-bottom flip in slow motion
- 20 times
- = Return-of-serve prep

### For big

- Shadow swings keeping racquet face **pointing at side fence** through whole loop
- If face wobbles → đang dùng wrist, not shoulder

## Decision tree tự xây

- **No time** = wrist flip
- **Time** = shoulder loop
- **Always set the face before ball arrives**
